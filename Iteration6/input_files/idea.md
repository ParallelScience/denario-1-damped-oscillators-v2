**Title: Perturbation-Based Robustness Analysis of Energy Dissipation Manifolds**

This study evaluates the sensitivity of the damped oscillator energy manifold to parametric uncertainty by quantifying the deviation between observed `total_energy` and theoretical energy dissipation models under controlled perturbations of `mass_kg` and `damping_coefficient`. By systematically perturbing these physical constants within their provided ranges, we calculate the sensitivity gradients of the energy decay residuals, $\Delta E = |E_{obs} - E_{model}(\tilde{m}, \tilde{b})|$, across the 20-oscillator population. This approach transforms the analytical parameters from fixed constants into variables, allowing us to map the stability of the energy conservation law against parameter estimation errors. We will utilize a vectorized grid search across the 20 oscillators to correlate these sensitivity profiles with specific damping ratios, identifying which regimes are most susceptible to energy divergence. The resulting sensitivity matrix will provide a rigorous assessment of how physical parameter precision impacts the reliability of long-term state prediction in underdamped systems.
