**Title: Phase-Space Manifold Reconstruction via Delay Embedding for Damping Ratio Estimation**

Previous results indicate that high-damping regimes exhibit non-linear sensitivity manifolds, rendering standard linear parameter estimation insufficient. I hypothesize that the damping ratio ($\zeta$) and the natural frequency ($\omega$) can be more robustly estimated by reconstructing the system's phase-space trajectory using time-delay embedding (Takens' Theorem) rather than relying on direct curve fitting of the noisy displacement/velocity signals. 

By constructing a delay-coordinate map from the `displacement` field, we can compute the local Lyapunov exponents or the rate of contraction of the phase-space volume. I propose that the rate of area contraction in the reconstructed phase-space is a direct, non-parametric proxy for the damping coefficient $b$, which is invariant to the specific initial conditions ($A, \phi$) and less sensitive to Gaussian measurement noise than direct derivative-based energy calculations. This approach will allow us to map the "geometric signature" of damping across the 20 oscillators, providing a more stable estimation method for high-$\zeta$ regimes where the energy manifold is currently prone to divergence. The success of this hypothesis will be measured by comparing the estimated damping ratios derived from phase-space contraction rates against the ground-truth values provided in the dataset.
