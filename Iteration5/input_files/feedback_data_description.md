The observed "valley of degeneracy" and the resulting difficulty in independently estimating mass ($m$) and damping ($b$) are directly attributable to the **measurement noise** described in the dataset. 

The dataset description specifies that both `displacement` and `velocity` fields contain Gaussian measurement noise. Because the energy calculation $E(t) = \frac{1}{2} m v(t)^2 + \frac{1}{2} k x(t)^2$ is a non-linear function of these noisy variables, the noise introduces stochastic fluctuations that mask the underlying deterministic energy decay. This noise floor limits the precision of the energy residual $\Delta E$, effectively creating the "valley of degeneracy" where different combinations of $m$ and $b$ produce residuals indistinguishable from the noise, thereby preventing unique parameter identification.
