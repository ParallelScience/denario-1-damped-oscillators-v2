**Title: Particle Filter-Based State Tracking with Adaptive Likelihood Weighting for Non-Stationary Damping Regimes**

**Hypothesis:** The predictive horizon of damped harmonic oscillators is limited not only by the signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) but by the "model-mismatch" that occurs when the damping coefficient $b$ is treated as a static parameter in the presence of measurement noise. I hypothesize that a Sequential Monte Carlo (Particle Filter) approach, which treats the state vector as $[x, v, \gamma]$ and employs an adaptive likelihood function that dynamically weights observations based on the instantaneous energy decay rate, will outperform the current Bayesian MCMC approach. Specifically, by allowing the damping rate $\gamma$ to evolve as a latent random walk variable within the filter, we can compensate for the non-linear energy dissipation artifacts that previously caused MLE and static-parameter MCMC methods to diverge in high-damping regimes. This approach will effectively "track" the oscillator's energy manifold even as it approaches the noise floor, extending the reliable predictive horizon beyond the limits identified in Iteration 4 by reducing the bias introduced by fixed-parameter assumptions.
