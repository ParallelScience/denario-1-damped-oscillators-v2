The current research has successfully optimized parameter estimation ($b, m$) and defined the Information Horizon ($T_H$) using Fisher Information. However, all previous iterations treat the oscillators as independent, isolated systems. I hypothesize that the "predictive state modeling" can be significantly improved by leveraging the latent correlation between oscillators. Specifically, I propose a **Hierarchical Bayesian State-Space Model (HB-SSM)** that treats the 20 oscillators as a population drawn from a shared hyper-prior distribution of physical parameters. By implementing a **Global-Local Shrinkage Prior** (e.g., Horseshoe prior) on the damping coefficients, we can perform "information borrowing" across the population. This will allow the model to stabilize parameter estimates for high-damping (low-SNR) oscillators by utilizing the more precise information gathered from low-damping (high-SNR) oscillators. I expect this to reduce the variance of $\hat{b}$ by at least 20% compared to the current individual Levenberg-Marquardt approach, particularly for oscillators with $\zeta > 0.7$, where individual estimation remains volatile due to rapid energy decay.
