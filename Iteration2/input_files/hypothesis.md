The "Information Horizon" is fundamentally constrained by the coupling between the phase-space trajectory and the measurement noise floor. I hypothesize that by implementing a Physics-Informed Extended Kalman Filter (PI-EKF) that utilizes the analytical damped harmonic oscillator model as the state transition function, we can dynamically re-weight the observation covariance matrix based on the instantaneous Relative Sensitivity Index $\tilde{S}(t)$. Specifically, by treating the damping coefficient $b$ and natural frequency $\omega$ as hidden states within the EKF, we can perform real-time parameter estimation that compensates for the identified increase in relative sensitivity as the system approaches equilibrium. This approach will outperform static parameter estimation by effectively "tracking" the physical parameters through the noise, thereby extending the predictive horizon $T_H$ beyond the limits imposed by fixed-parameter models, particularly in the high-damping regime ($\zeta > 0.5$) where the signal-to-noise ratio degrades most rapidly.
